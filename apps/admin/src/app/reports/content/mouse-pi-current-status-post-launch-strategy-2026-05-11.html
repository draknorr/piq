05 / Store page

Optimization options

Store-page improvements should make the promise clearer and the support story more credible. The page does not need more words; it needs a sharper order of proof.

High confidence Strategic inference
Area Recommended change Why it matters
First-screen promise Lead with "1930s cartoon noir FPS" and a player-facing action promise before broader genre descriptors. The identity is the differentiator; generic retro-shooter language pulls MOUSE into a crowded comparison set.
Trailer and screenshot order Front-load a sequence that proves style, combat feel, detective fantasy, and mission variety in that order. Players already understand the visual hook. The next job is proving that the game sustains it.
Post-launch support block Add or refresh copy around the major patch: smoother controls, bug cleanup, quest handling, and clearer progression. Converts support work into purchase reassurance.
Deluxe / DLC clarity State what Deluxe includes, what future story DLC means, and when the next timing update will arrive. Prevents future-content ambiguity from becoming a trust issue.
Review and quote selection Prefer quotes that combine identity with playability, not only praise for the look. The conversion gap is whether the unique style also delivers a satisfying game.
Evaluation lens A

Style-first positioning

Test whether the store's first impression makes the hand-drawn cartoon/noir identity unmistakable before it moves into genre detail.

Evaluation lens B

Case-first motivation

Evaluate whether the page gives players a concrete reason to care about the detective fantasy after the visual hook lands.

Evaluation lens C

Support-first reassurance

Check whether the page makes post-launch responsiveness visible enough for owners, wishlisters, and hesitant buyers.

06 / Timing

Post-launch beat sequence

The next three public beats should be separated. Patch first, reactivation second, DLC roadmap third.

Directional signal Strategic inference
May 18-20

Primary patch window

Lead with quality, controls, bug cleanup, quest handling, and late cleanup clarity. This is the strongest near-term window before more crowded overlap weeks.

Jun 2-4

Backup patch window

Use only if the patch is not ready in May. Keep the same message hierarchy and avoid turning the update into a technical changelog.

Jun 15-17

Reactivation beat

Give case-closed players a light return goal: tracked cleanup, collectibles, community challenge, or visible progress wins.

Aug 10-11

DLC roadmap window

Clarify entitlement, timing band, and creative promise. This should read as trust-building, not pressure.

Avoid highly crowded release weeks where lookalike FPS titles and attention-adjacent games make it harder for a support or DLC message to stand out. Use the earliest patch window only if the update can credibly include the player-facing improvements named in this report.
